PROBLEM

Crises, like bank robberies or school shootings, are extremely dangerous and require urgent help from professionals such as the police or paramedics. Often times, reinforcements arrive to the scene later than we would like them to, or they enter with the wrong strategy. This can lead to an increase in injuries or death.

SOLUTION

Our intention is to create a portable device that is easy to carry and allows people to quickly alert the authorities that help is needed and what kind of a situation they are in. The police could plan the best approach depending on the circumstances. This device would be useful to teachers and staff in schools or any authority figures in general, but is still available to citizens above the age of 18. In other words, this device would not be made available to minors.

HOW IT WORKS

CrisisWear is a security button device in the form of accessories like bracelets, necklaces, keychains, etc. They can be activated when the button on the side of the main surface is pushed twice consecutively. Then, you would be able to push the button a certain number of times depending on your current situation. For this product, one push signals an armed attacker and two pushes are for when the user is unsure of the situation. This would allow the police to be alerted of the situation you are in and where you are. It would also let the police plan out how to arrive to the scene and how to handle the situation. For example, if it was a school shooting, the police would want to arrive discreetly, whereas if a robbery is in progress, it might be different. The key/legend of how many times you should push the button for each circumstance would be engraved into the keychain/pendant. The device would connect to your phone and send a message to the nearest police department and send the coordinates of the device to them as well through the GPS feature.

INSPIRATION

We were first inspired to make CrisisWear when we looked through a document listing problems in the world ranging from local to global. We noticed how a lot of people thought that public crimes such as school shootings or bank robberies were a rising problem in the world today. Although we were not able to think of a realistic way to prevent these crimes, we realized that we could do something about how quickly and strategically the police and/or paramedics arrive at the scene after these kinds of situations. For example, in the Florida school shooting this February, the fire alarm was set off, making it easier for the shooter to do harm since everyone was outside in one big group. The police were most likely misinformed about the situation and had the materials and backup to handle a fire, not a school shooting. This was the inspiration behind our idea to make sure that the owner of our device could alert the police department on the situation taking place and the location so that they would be better equipped to handle it. In addition, we wanted our device to come in the form of accessories to make them blend in and portable.
