PROBLEM

Crises, like bank robberies or school shootings, are extremely dangerous and require urgent help from professionals such as the police or paramedics. Often times, reinforcements arrive to the scene later than we would like them to, or they enter with the wrong strategy. This can lead to an increase in injuries or death

SOLUTION

Our intention is to create a portable device that is easy to carry and allows people to quickly alert the authorities that help is needed, and what kind of a situation they are in. Therefore, the police could plan the best approach depending on the circumstances. This device would be useful to teachers and staff in schools or any authority figures in general, but is still available to citizens. This device is would not be made available to minors.

HOW IT WORKS

CrisisWear is a device in the form of accessories like bracelets, necklaces, keychains, and etc., that can be activated when a button on the side of it is pushed five times consecutively. Then, you would be able to push the button again a certain number of times depending on your current situation. For example, one push signals a shooting, two indicates a robbery, and etc. This would allow the police to not only be alerted of your condition quickly, but it would also let the police plan out how to arrive at the scene and how to handle the situation. For example, if it was a school shooting, the police would want to arrive quietly and discreetly, whereas after a robbery has occurred, it would not matter how quiet or loud the police was. The key/legend of how many times you should push the button for each circumstance would be given to you when you receive the product and you could memorize it, attatch it to your ID badge, store it in your pocket, and more.

INSPIRATION

We were first inspired to make CrisisWear when we looked through a document listing problems in the world ranging from local to global. We noticed how a lot of people thought that public crimes such as school shootings or bank robberies were a rising problem in the world today. Although we were not able to think of a realistic way to prevent these crimes, we realized that we could do something about how quickly and strategically the police and/or paramedics arrive at the scene after these kinds of situations. For example, in the Florida school shooting this February, the fire alarm was set off, making it easier for the shooter to do harm since everyone was outside in one big group. The police were most likely misinformed about the situation and had the materials and backup to handle a fire, not a school shooting. This was the inspiration behind our idea to make sure that the owner of our device could alert the police department on the situation taking place so that they would be better equipped to handle it. In addition, we wanted our device to come in the form of accessories so that they would blend in, be portable, and look fashionable.
